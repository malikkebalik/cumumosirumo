How did John Prine start his Professional Career?

John Prine’s professional career began in 1971 with the publication of his self-titled début album.

Kristofferson discovered Prine when he brought him to New York to perform at the Bitter End; the following morning, Atlantic Records offered him a $25,000 contract.

In 1972, he released “Diamonds In The Rough” followed by “Sweet Revenge” in 1973. “Common Sense” was published in 1975 and became Prine’s first album to be charted in the US Top 100 by Billboard.

In the same year, he toured North America with a complete ensemble that included guitarist Arlen Roth. Prine released “Bruised Orange” and “Pink Cadillac” as his fifth and sixth albums, respectively.

Along with his associate Al Bunetta, he co-founded the independent record label “Oh Boy Records” in 1981.

The Grammy Award-winning album “The Missing Years” was published by Prine, along with “Lost Dogs and Mixed Blessings” (1995) and “Despite Ourselves” (1999).

In 2001, Prine co-starred in “Daddy & Them” with Billy Bob Thornton. 2005 saw the publication of Prine’s fifteenth album, “Fair & Square,” which garnered the Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Folk Album in 2005.

Oh Boy Records released “Broken Hearts and Dirty Windows: The Songs of John Prine” in 2010.

In 2016, John Prine was awarded the PEN/Song Lyrics Award. Prine won Artist of the Year at the 2017 Americana Music Honors & Awards for the second time, having previously won in 2005.

On April 13, 2013, he released his first new album in thirteen years, titled “The Tree of Forgiveness” The album achieved Prine’s highest Billboard 200 chart position.
