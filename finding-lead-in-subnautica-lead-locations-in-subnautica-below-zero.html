Finding Lead in Subnautica: Lead Locations in Subnautica Below Zero

Publish date: 2024-07-04

Understanding Lead in Subnautica Below Zero

Lead is a valuable material in Subnautica, primarily obtained from Sandstone Outcrops. It is essential for crafting items like Radiation Suits and Reactor Rods, offering protection against radiation. Apart from small quantities, lead can also be mined from large resource deposits. Key uses of Lead include:

  • Utilized in various Crafting recipes.

  • Incorporated into fabricator recipes.

  • A component essential for constructing structures with the Habitat Builder.

  • Used in vehicle construction at the Mobile Vehicle Bay.

In earlier game versions, lead needed to be crafted using the Fabricator, with specific recipes varying. Initially, crafting lead necessitated three Copper Ore and one Silver Ore, while a subsequent recipe involved two Batteries to create “Lead Plate.” Initially, Lead was extracted from Limestone Outcrops before it became a natural resource.

Lead Acquisition in Subnautica

Locating Sandstone Outcrops is the key to obtaining Lead in Subnautica. These outcrops, small rocks that can be broken open for resources, offer a 25% chance of gold, 37.5% chance of silver, and a 37.5% chance of lead. Lead is commonly found in rocks near the Sandstone Outcrops, prevalent in beginner-friendly areas such as Safe Shallows and kelp forests.

Discovering Lead in Subnautica Below Zero

In Subnautica Below Zero, Lead can be found in different biomes, especially in Galena Outcrops. These biomes include:

  • Twisty Bridges
  • Crystal Caves
  • Lilypad Islands
  • Glacial Connection
  • Thermal Spires
  • Shallow Twisty Bridges
  • East Arctic

    • The Twisty Bridges biome is an ideal spot to find Galena Outcrops, especially near the Drop Pod, offering a safe location for early-game base setups.

    Effective Lead Retrieval in Subnautica Below Zero

    To secure lead efficiently in the Twisty Bridges biome:

  • Prioritize acquiring a High Capacity O2 Tank for extended exploration.

  • Equip a Rebreather for dives below 100 meters.

  • Use a Seaglider for faster swimming.

  • (Optional) Consider a Seatruck for expedited movement.

  • Employ a Mineral Detector for easy Galena Outcrop identification.

    • Explore caverns and channels in Twisty Bridges biome, focusing on areas deeper than 100 meters for lead retrieval using the Rebreather. Increase your chances by using a Mineral Detector and following these handy tips:

    • Explore during daylight for better visibility.

    • Check cliffs and rocky cuts in the terrain where Outcrops hide.

    • Create a Mineral Detector using specific components.

    • Crack open multiple Outcrops for Titanium or Lead.

    • Twisty Bridges biome is the most accessible and secure location for lead compared to other biomes in Subnautica Below Zero.
