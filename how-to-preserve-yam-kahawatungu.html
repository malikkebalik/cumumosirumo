How To Preserve Yam - KahawaTungu

Publish date: 2024-08-15

Yam, a versatile and nutritious tuber, is a staple food in many cultures around the world. However, yams are highly perishable and require proper preservation techniques to extend their shelf life. In this guide, we’ll explore various methods how to preserve yam to ensure their freshness and flavor for an extended period, allowing you to enjoy this delicious ingredient year-round.

  • Selecting Fresh Yams
    • Start with selecting fresh, firm yams that are free from bruises, blemishes, or soft spots.
    • Choose yams with smooth, unblemished skins and vibrant color, indicating ripeness and quality.
  • Cleaning and Preparation
    • Wash the yams thoroughly under cold running water to remove any dirt or debris.
    • Use a clean brush to scrub the yams gently, paying special attention to crevices and areas with stubborn dirt.
    • Pat the yams dry with a clean kitchen towel to remove excess moisture before proceeding with preservation.
  • Storing Yams at Room Temperature
    • Yams can be stored at room temperature in a cool, dark, and well-ventilated area, such as a pantry or cellar.
    • Arrange the yams in a single layer on a shelf or in a basket, ensuring good air circulation to prevent moisture buildup.
    • Avoid exposing yams to direct sunlight or high temperatures, as this can cause them to sprout or spoil prematurely.
  • Refrigerating Yams
    • If you prefer to refrigerate yams for short-term storage, place them in a perforated plastic bag or vegetable drawer in the refrigerator.
    • Keep the temperature between 45°F and 50°F (7°C to 10°C) to maintain optimal freshness and prevent sprouting.
    • Consume refrigerated yams within 1 to 2 weeks to ensure the best flavor and texture.
  • Freezing Yams
    • To freeze yams for long-term preservation, start by peeling and cutting them into uniform pieces or slices.
    • Blanch the yam pieces in boiling water for 3 to 5 minutes, then transfer them to an ice bath to stop the cooking process.
    • Drain the blanched yams thoroughly and pat them dry with a kitchen towel before placing them in airtight freezer bags or containers.
    • Label the bags or containers with the date and store them in the freezer at 0°F (-18°C) or below for up to 10 to 12 months.
  • Dehydrating Yams
    • Another method for preserving yams is dehydration, which involves removing moisture to inhibit bacterial growth and spoilage.
    • Peel and slice the yams into thin, uniform slices using a sharp knife or mandoline slicer.
    • Arrange the yam slices in a single layer on dehydrator trays, ensuring space between each slice for proper air circulation.
    • Dehydrate the yam slices at 125°F (52°C) for 6 to 12 hours, or until they are thoroughly dried and crisp.
    • Once dehydrated, store the yam chips in airtight containers or vacuum-sealed bags in a cool, dry, and dark pantry for up to 6 to 12 months.
