Who is Patti Smith?

Patricia Lee Smith known as Patti Smith is an American singer-songwriter. She is known for being an influential component of the New York City punk rock movement with her 1975 debut album Horses.

Similarly, she was called the punk poet laureate, she fused rock and poetry in her work.

Patti Smith: Age, Parents, Siblings, Ethnicity, Nationality

She was born on 30th December 1946 in Chicago, Illinois, US. Similarly, she is the daughter of Grant Smith and Beverly Smith.

Her father was a machinist at a Honeywell plant and her mother was a jazz singer who turned waitress. She was the eldest of four children and she has a brother named Todd Smith.

Similarly, her nationality is American and her ethnicity is Irish, English, Welsh, and Cornish.

Talking about her education, she graduated from Deptford Township High School in the year 1964. Similarly, she went to Glassboro State College.

Patti Smith: Professional Life and  Career

Talking about her career, she is an American singer-songwriter. She became an influential component of the New York City punk rock movement with her 1975 debut album Horses.

Similarly, she was called the “punk poet laureate” as she fused rock and poetry in her work.

Also, Patti Smith’s most widely known song is “Because of the Night”. It was co-written with Bruce Springsteen.

Similarly, it reached number 13 on the billboard hot 100 charts in the year 1978 and number five in the U.K. In the year 2005, she was named a Commander of the Ordre des Arts et des Lettres by the French Ministry of Culture.

Likewise, in the year 2007, she was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. After that, on November 17, 2010, she won the National Book Award for her memoir Just Kids.

Furthermore, the books fulfilled a promise she had made to her former long-time roommate and partner Robert Mapplethorpe.

Moreover, she placed 47th in Rolling Stone magazine’s list of 100 Greatest Artists published in December 2010 and was also a recipient of the 2011 Polar Music Prize.
