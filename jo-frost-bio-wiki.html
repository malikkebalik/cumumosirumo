Who is Jo Frost?

Jo Frost is a British television personality who rose to fame through the Channel 4 hit TV show Supernanny.

After that, she worked on several other reality shows in the United Kingdom, the United States, and the Netherlands.

She has over 25 years of experience as a childcare professional.

Jo Frost: Age, Parent, Ethnicity

Jo Frost was born on 27 June 1971 in London, United Kingdom. Her parents’ names are Michael Frost and Joa Frost. As of 2023, she is 50 years old.

Jo’s father was an English builder and her mother, born in Gibraltar, was an interior decorator. Her mother died of cancer in 1994.

Jo Frost also has a brother named Matthew. Being born in England, she holds British nationality along with a mixed (Danish, Icelandic, Norwegian) ethnicity.

Jo Frost: Professional life, Career

Jo Frost started her career in the year 1989 when she was just 18 years old. She joined Channel 4’s reality show ‘Supernanny’ in 2004.

This show was watched by over six million viewers in the very first year and thus, it became a huge success.

Firstly it was aired on the UK version and after great success, the show was also re-created in several other countries.

After working for about seven years in the show, she quit ‘Supernanny’ and another woman was hired in place of her. Jo Frost was nominated for the Favorite Nanny award at the TV Land Awards, in 2008.

After this, she appeared on another show ‘Jo Frost: Extreme Parental Guidance, in the year 2010.

Three years later, Jo Frost worked in ‘Family S.O.S. with Jo Frost’. That show shows different issues regarding addiction, abuse, blended families, marital problems, etc.

And in the year 2014, Jo Frost hosted ITV’s ‘Family Matters. A year later,  Nanny Jo Productions worked with Strix Television and started the show ‘Nanny on Tour.

Nine Network announced Frost would front their new show The Parent Jury in Australia on September 16, 2020.

As An Author

Jo Frost has written six books about parenting and child care.

These books include: ‘Supernanny: How to Get the Best from Your Children, ‘Ask Supernanny: What Every Parent Wants to Know, ‘Jo Frost’s Confident Baby Care’, ‘Jo Frost’s Toddler SOS’, ‘Jo Frost’s Toddler Rules’ and ‘Jo Frost’s Confident Toddler Care.

As An Advocate

Jo Frost is also an advocate for the UN foundation’s ‘Shot@Life’. It was a movement aiming to reduce vaccine-preventable childhood diseases and deaths.

Moreover, she worked with people who suffer from life-threatening allergies for their improvement.

Frost is also the 2014 ambassador for FARE (full form: Food, Allergy, Research, and Education). Furthermore, she is also an activist for kids dealing with abandonment and poverty.
