Father Seydou &amp; Mother Fatimata

Publish date: 2024-08-14

Ousmane Dembélé was born on 15 May 1997 in Vernon, Eure, in Normandy to his parents Seydou and Fatimata Dembélé.

He has a fascinating multicultural background, with his mother hailing from Waly Diantang, a town known for its Mauritanian-Senegalese heritage, while his father originates from Mali.

His early soccer journey began in the town of Évreux, where he first honed his skills at ALM Évreux, and later at Évreux FC 27, between the formative ages of 12 and 13.

This nurturing environment played a crucial role in shaping his promising career in the world of soccer.

Ousmane Dembélé Siblings

Ousmane Dembele, the talented soccer player, has a brother and a sister, but their names and other details remain undisclosed.

This has led to confusion among people, as there are numerous soccer players with the surname Dembele.

In contrast to his siblings, Ousmane chose a different path.

His love for soccer led him to turn away from formal education, a decision supported by his family, who recognized his immense passion and talent.

While his brother and sister pursued their studies, Ousmane was granted the opportunity to chase his dreams in the world of soccer.

At the tender age of six, Ousmane embarked on a journey from his birthplace, Vernon in Northern France, to Rennes in North-East France, where he met his uncle Sambagué, a soccer player and model figure for young Ousmane.

Under his uncle’s guidance, Ousmane received valuable lessons and formally began his youth soccer career at the Madeleine Évreux soccer club in 2004.

Throughout his career, Ousmane looked up to local idols, drawing inspiration from their achievements.

PSG Transfer

Barcelona’s head coach, Xavi Hernandez, expressed his disappointment with Ousmane Dembele after confirming that the forward has informed him of his desire to join Paris Saint-Germain, as reported by ESPN last week.

As negotiations between Barcelona and PSG continue, they are working on finalizing the fee for the transfer, which is anticipated to be approximately €50 million ($54.9m).

The deal is on the verge of completion and is expected to be wrapped up in the coming days.

Speculations have emerged suggesting that if the transfer to PSG is agreed upon, Barcelona will consider themselves fortunate to be rid of what some consider to be the worst signing in the club’s history.

Dembele’s time at Barcelona has been marred by injuries and inconsistent performances, leading to disappointment among fans and club officials alike.
