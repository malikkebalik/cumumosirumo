Barry Diller Wikipedia and Biography

Barry Diller Wikipedia: -Barry Diller was born on February 2, 1942, in San Francisco, California, U.S. Diller is an American businessman. He is Chairman and Senior Executive of IAC and Expedia Group and founded the Fox Broadcasting Company and USA Broadcasting. After dropping out of UCLA after three weeks, Diller began his work through a familial connection in the mailroom of the William Morris Agency. Because he was close to the company's file room, he could spend his leisure time browsing through the archives and learning about the whole history of the entertainment business.  Elton Rule, then the West Coast president of ABC, recruited him as an assistant and advanced him to network President at the same time Diller joined him in 1964, transferring him to New York City.

Career

Barry Diller Career: - From 1974 until 1984, Diller was the chairman and chief executive officer of Paramount Pictures Corporation. The studio produced hit television shows such as Laverne & Shirley (1976), Taxi (1978), and Cheers (1982), as well as films such as Saturday Night Fever (1977), Grease (1978), Raiders of the Lost Ark (1981) and sequel Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom (1984), Terms of Endearment (1983), and Beverly Hills Cop (1984) with Diller at the helm.

Diller was also "one of the three key executives" for then-Paramount parent company Gulf+Western, according to the New York Times in 1983, with A. D. Murphy, director of the motion picture producing program at the University of Southern California, describing him as "probably the most successful executive in the film business today."Diller indicated in the same piece, among other things, that he wants to utilise Paramount to develop films based on games supplied by Sega, the then Gulf+Western-owned video game manufacturing business. Diller, as CEO of Paramount, also organised a three-way ownership of the USA Network in 1981 to prevent the Gulf+Western-owned Madison Square Garden from selling its stake in the network.
