Megan Thee Stallion's boyfriend, Pardi, Shares Support as Jury Deliberations Begin

Publish date: 2024-08-17

Megan Thee Stallion’s partner, Pardison ‘Pardi’ Fontaine, expressed his support for women as jury deliberations commenced in the assault case against Tory Lanez.

The 32-year-old songwriter shared words of empathy on his Instagram Story for women, especially those of color, who have experienced injustice and the ridicule that often follows when they speak up.

Pardi emphasized the challenges women face when their credibility is questioned, and their past is scrutinized. He also mentioned the swift transition from victim to defendant in the eyes of the public.

He concluded by expressing his prayers for the protection of women, highlighting the unfortunate situation and the impact it can have on their lives.

During the court proceedings, Megan shared how the incident has led to depression, affecting her relationship with her partner.

She expressed feeling ashamed and disgusted, highlighting the immense impact the ordeal has had on her life.

Jury deliberations have commenced in the trial, with Lanez facing up to 18 years in prison if found guilty.

The jury, consisting of seven women and five men, will need to reach a unanimous decision for a guilty verdict on any of the three charges Lanez is facing.

The trial has garnered attention, with Lanez’s defense team presenting a theory that deflects the blame away from him and onto Megan’s friend, Kelsey Harris.

Amidst the legal battle, Lanez has pleaded not guilty to the charges and continues to maintain his innocence.
