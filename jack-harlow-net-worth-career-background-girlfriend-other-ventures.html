Career

Soon after, he was introduced to DJ Drama, whose record label Generation Now he later joined. Harlow later released his major label debut mixtape, “Loose.” He released the single “Thru the Night” and the mixtape “Confetti” in 2019.

Harlow achieved commercial success in January 2020 with the release of “Whats Poppin,” the lead single from his EP “Sweet Action.” The song, which was widely shared on TikTok, reached number two on the Billboard Hot 100 when it was rereleased as a remix in June, and its music video received over 100 million views. Furthermore, “Whats Poppin” received a Grammy nomination for Best Rap Performance. Harlow’s success continued with the singles “Moana,” featuring G-Eazy, and “Tyler Herro.”

Other Ventures

Harlow is active in a variety of charitable and activist causes. He attended a Black Lives Matter rally in 2020 to protest the police killing of Breonna Taylor in his hometown of Louisville. The following year, Harlow made donations to several Louisville-area organizations, including the Center for Women and Families, Metro United Way, and the Grace M. James Academy of Excellence. He also collaborated with KFC and its parent company Yum! Brands to donate a quarter-million dollars to the American Red Cross to assist those affected by a deadly tornado in Western Kentucky.

Background

Jack Harlow was born on March 13, 1998 in Louisville, Kentucky to Brian and Maggie Harlow. He grew up on a horse farm with his younger brother, Clayborn. Harlow’s ancestors are Irish and French. He began rapping at the age of 12 and used a microphone and laptop to record beats with his friend Copeland.

The boys collaborated on a CD called “Rippin’ and Rappin’,” which they sold at Highland Middle School. Harlow made his first mixtape, “Extra Credit,” in seventh grade. He also formed the Moose Gang with his friends. Throughout high school, Harlow sold out shows at Louisville venues such as Mercury Ballroom and the Haymarket Whiskey Bar.
