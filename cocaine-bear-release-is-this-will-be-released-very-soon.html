About Cocaine Bear Movie

Given the prodigious quantity of literary ability in our global creative lake, it can frequently come as a surprise when, outside of documentaries, we realize that a particular tale is real. Yet occasionally the best and funniest tales can only come from truth, and Cocaine Bear is one of those tales.

Of course, specifics surrounding the plot’s originality have yet to be determined, but it would be reasonable to assume the writers may have stayed true to reality given this extraordinary true story.

The story of Cocaine Bear begins with an incident that happened in December 1985. The pilot of the plane was a notorious and previously convicted drug smuggler who also happened to be a former narcotics officer. He threw a duffel bag filled with cocaine out the window of the plane in an effort to lighten his weight.

The pilot, Andrew C. Thornton II, then made the decision to pursue his dropped cargo, but when he tried to deploy his parachute, he was unable and fell to his death below. Even while it may already seem like a crazy story, this is really just the beginning. After falling into the hands of a 175-pound American black bear, who decided to eat it, the duffel bag of cocaine, which was valued at over $15 million USD, was consumed.

The story of the movie is probably going to follow what happened to the bear that, while under this tremendous spell, went on a murdering rampage. The rest of the story has been well documented because it is non-fiction, but it might be best to stop there in order to prevent any potential movie spoilers.
